ABORTION SUPPORT

Care that protects your autonomy before, during, and after a procedure.

You deserve a therapist who trusts your abortion decision and helps you untangle the noise around it. Clients come to these sessions carrying layered realities: restrictive laws, travel burdens, secrecy, grief, relief, or the need to decide if now is even the right time.

In our telehealth space we slow everything down. We name the political pressure, religious trauma, or family rhetoric that might be shaping how you feel. We track how your nervous system is responding, identify what safety looks like for you, and outline the practical supports—funds, travel buddies, medication plans, rest time—that make this experience safer.

If you are still deciding, we explore your values without fear, shame, or coercion. If you have a procedure scheduled, we build scripts, boundaries, and sensory grounding tools so you stay in control. If you are recovering, we create room for complicated emotions, physical healing, and the meaning-making that follows.

Every session is collaborative. You set the pace, and I stay present with humor, affirmation, and trauma-informed care so you can leave feeling steadier, resourced, and seen.

You deserve abortion support that dismantles shame and centers your lived experience.

Abortion can be logistical, medical, emotional, and political all at once. Many Texans are traveling long distances, keeping secrets, or navigating unsupportive systems just to receive basic healthcare. That constant vigilance shows up in your body—tight chest, racing thoughts, numbness, or exhaustion from holding it together for everyone else.

Inside our sessions you can finally set the load down, speak honestly about what this experience is costing you, and hear it reflected back without judgment. We honor the reasons you're here whether this is a first-time decision, miscarriage management, or ending a wanted pregnancy. We also hold space for identities facing added barriers—queer and trans clients, BIPOC communities, immigrants, people with disabilities, and survivors of violence—so you never have to translate or defend yourself.

  • Validate every emotion—relief, grief, anger, numbness—without moralizing or ranking them.
  • Name systemic harm tied to gender, race, immigration status, disability, or class so blame doesn't land on you.
  • Co-create sensory and somatic grounding tools that support medication or procedural care.
  • Coordinate practical next steps like rest plans, boundary scripts, and referrals to trusted funds and clinics.

You leave each session with clarity, next steps, and permission to take up the space you need to heal.
