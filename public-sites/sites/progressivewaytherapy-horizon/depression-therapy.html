What Changes

DEPRESSION THERAPY

We co-create relief that fits your energy, culture, and realities.

Depression can feel like moving through molasses. You're exhausted, numb, irritable, or checked out—but life keeps demanding more. Therapy becomes a place to exhale, name what hurts, and gently build momentum.

We focus on nervous-system regulation, compassionate accountability, and dismantling oppressive narratives that feed burnout. We celebrate small wins, honor rest, and design routines that work with (not against) your body.

We start with small, doable shifts—not perfection.

Your homework might be drinking water, texting a friend, or practicing a two-minute grounding exercise. We remix coping tools until they feel actually usable, even on low-energy days.

  • Identify the beliefs and systems that keep you stuck in shame or over-functioning.
  • Map your energy rhythms so we can plan rest, movement, and nourishment that feel realistic.
  • Develop scripts for asking for help, setting boundaries, or communicating needs without guilt.

We aim for steadier mornings, softer evenings, and more moments where you feel like you again.

Where depression therapy focuses

Burnout & executive dysfunction

Build pacing, task-breakdown, and body double practices that help you function without self-abandonment.

Identity & oppression fatigue

Process how racism, queerphobia, misogyny, or ableism shape your mood, and craft liberatory responses.

Seasonal & cyclical lows

Plan rituals, light, movement, and support stacks for the seasons or cycles where symptoms spike.

Hopelessness & self-worth

Rewrite internal narratives, reconnect to purpose, and build self-compassion practices you can believe in.
